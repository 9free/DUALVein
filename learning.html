Vein Anatomy & Physiology

Key concepts every nursing student must know:

  • 🔹 Veins vs Arteries – Veins have thinner walls, valves, and lower pressure
  • 🔹 Best sites: Cephalic, Basilic, Median Cubital veins (antecubital fossa)
  • 🔹 Avoid: Joints, areas with valves, scarred or thrombosed veins
  • 🔹 DIVA score – Difficult Intravenous Access assessment tool
  • 🔹 Skin tone considerations – Near-infrared (DUALVein) helps visualize deep veins

Common IV Complications & Troubleshooting

Phlebitis

Inflammation of the vein. Signs: redness, warmth, pain.

Prevention: Proper site rotation, aseptic technique.

Infiltration / Extravasation

Fluid leaks into surrounding tissue.

Action: Stop infusion immediately, elevate limb.

Occlusion / Rolling Veins

Most common beginner mistake.

Tip: Anchor vein properly + use DUALVein visualization.

Evidence-Based Guidelines (2024–2025)

Latest from Infusion Nurses Society (INS) 9th Edition

  • Limit attempts to 2 per clinician
  • Use ultrasound/near-infrared visualization for difficult access
  • Standardized skin antisepsis with CHG-alcohol
