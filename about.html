About DUALVein

Built to close the gap between training and reality.

We're a team of engineers and researchers from CUHK, working to make IV training safer, more accessible, and more realistic for everyone.

Our Story

Why DUALVein exists

In 2024, DUALVein was born from a simple observation: medical students train on mannequins with perfect, visible veins — but real patients are nothing like that. We witnessed nursing and medical students struggle during their first real IV insertions. The gap between classroom training and clinical reality was causing unnecessary pain for patients and anxiety for new healthcare professionals.

We knew there had to be a better way — one that was affordable enough for schools, accurate enough for real use, and simple enough for any student to pick up and go.

Direction

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To provide affordable, accessible, and accurate vein visualization technology that bridges the gap between classroom learning and real-world clinical practice — ultimately reducing patient discomfort and improving healthcare outcomes.

Our Vision

A future where every nursing and medical student has access to realistic training tools. A world where failed IV attempts are rare, patient pain is minimized, and healthcare professionals feel confident from day one.

What We Stand For

Our Values

Innovation

We push boundaries in medical education technology, constantly seeking better ways to prepare healthcare professionals for real-world complexity.

Accessibility

Quality training tools should be available to all institutions — not just the best-funded ones. Affordability is a design requirement, not an afterthought.

Patient Safety

Every decision we make is driven by one goal: fewer failed attempts, less pain, and better outcomes for patients at every stage of care.

Our Journey

2024 Q2

Project Inception

DUALVein concept developed in response to observed training gaps in medical education at CUHK.

Prototype Development

First working prototype completed with AI-powered vein detection algorithm achieving 75% accuracy across diverse skin tones.

Ongoing pilot deployments at partner institutions and continued product development based on real-world feedback from students and educators.
